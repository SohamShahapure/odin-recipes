White Sauce Pasta

Description

Pasta, the heart and soul of Italian cuisine, is more than just a meal; it’s a celebration of culture, tradition, and taste. Originating from Italy, this beloved staple is crafted with simplicity and precision, primarily from durum wheat flour and water. Its beauty lies in its diversity, with each shape and size designed to pair perfectly with various sauces, creating a symphony of flavors that transport you straight to the rolling hills and vibrant piazzas of Italy.

Italian cuisine is known for its authenticity and simplicity, where each ingredient sings in harmony. In dishes like Spaghetti Carbonara, the creamy, savory blend of eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper showcases the elegance of straightforward ingredients combined with skill. Penne Arrabbiata, with its fiery tomato sauce and hint of garlic and red chili flakes, offers a passionate burst of flavor that’s quintessentially Italian.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons margarine
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cube chicken bouillon, crumbled.
  • 1 1/2 cups boiling water.
  • 1 cup 2 % milk
  • ground white pepper to taste.

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Melt butter and margerine in saucepan over medium-low heat.
  3. Add flour and crumbled bouillon; stir untill well-blend , 1 to 2 mins.
  4. Continue to cook and stir until thickened and lightly browned, about 5 minutes.
  5. Increase the heat to medium and whisk in water until smooth.
  6. Stir in milk; cook and stir until hot and thickened, 2 to 3 more minutes.
  7. Season with white pepper.
